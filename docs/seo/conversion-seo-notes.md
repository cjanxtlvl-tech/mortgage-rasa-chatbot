# Conversion SEO Notes

## Matching Page Intent To CTA
- Align CTA language with query intent.
- Example: informational page CTA should be "Talk To A Loan Specialist" rather than immediate full application.
- Transactional pages should prioritize "Get Pre-Approved" or "Call Now".

## Short Mobile Forms
- Keep initial forms short (name, contact, loan goal, timeline).
- Defer non-essential fields to follow-up.
- Mobile users often call instead of filling long forms, so always include click-to-call.

## Click-To-Call Placement
- Place `tel:` CTA in header and near key decision sections.
- Repeat call CTA after major trust or comparison blocks.
- Track all `tel:` taps as `phone_click`.

## Above-The-Fold Messaging
- State value proposition quickly: who you help, where you serve, what next step is.
- Keep first-screen content focused on borrower intent and conversion action.
- Avoid generic hero messaging that delays user decisions.

## Trust Signals
- Add verified reviews, licensing details, and service transparency.
- Show local credibility where accurate (team presence, service areas, case examples).
- Place trust signals adjacent to forms and call CTAs.

## Reducing Friction
- Reduce navigation clutter on conversion-focused landing pages.
- Use clear labels and inline validation for form fields.
- Avoid intrusive popups that block form or call actions.

## Speed And Bounce Rate
- Improve load speed for money pages first.
- Defer non-essential scripts and optimize media.
- Investigate high bounce pages with session replay + event data.

## Content Clarity
- Use plain language for rates, terms, and eligibility.
- Break long sections into scannable blocks with clear headings.
- Tie educational content to next-step CTA.

## Internal Linking To Money Pages
- Link informational and FAQ content to pre-qual and consultation pages.
- Add contextual internal links near moments of high intent.
- Keep anchor text clear (for example, "Get Pre-Approved In Austin").

## Measuring SEO Conversion Quality
- Compare leads by source page, device category, and lead action type.
- Separate raw lead volume from qualified lead outcomes.
- Prioritize pages producing qualified calls/forms, not only high sessions.

## Practical rules for mortgage lead-gen pages
- Always include at least one visible call path and one form path on mobile.
- Keep first conversion step simple and low-friction.
- Match CTA copy to page intent and funnel stage.
- Use trust proof near conversion actions, not only in footer.
- Validate tracking before and after deployment to avoid blind spots.
